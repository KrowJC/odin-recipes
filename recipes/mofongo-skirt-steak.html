Mofongo with Skirt Steak

Introduction & History

Green plantain, pork cracklings chicharrón, garlic, and salt is all you need to make mofongo. However, there is, thankfully, more than one way to make it. Often, olive oil or chicken broth is added to moisten the mixture a bit, and sometimes a ripe plantain is used instead of a green one, while the use of either cassava, taro, eddoe, breadfruit, or a mixture of plantains and cassava instead of plantains is also quite widespread. Also, mofongo can have a seafood or a meat filling — alternatively, these can be just poured over a mofongo — in which case we are talking of mofongo relleno. The preparation starts with frying the plantain slices until golden, then mashing them in a pilón, a special mortar from Puerto Rico. Once mashed a bit, garlic, pork cracklings, and salt are added, and the mixture is mashed some more until well combined. The mixture is then shaped into a dome and typically served with fried meat on top and broth soup on the side.


Main Ingredients

  • Plantains
  • Chicharron
  • Garlic
  • Salt
  • Olive oil

Cooking Tips

Method

  1. When frying the plantains, the temperature should never be above 350°F as the plantains need to cook throughout.
  2. They should also be fried only until lightly golden otherwise they will be too dry to mash.
  3. For mashing the plantains, if you do not own a pilón, you can use a potato masher instead.
  4. The garlic should be pounded to a paste before it's added to the plantains, unlike mofongo which should never be completely be mashed into a paste.
  5. A bit of broth or olive oil can be added as well to help moisten the mixture a bit if necessary.

Serving

  1. Serve with fried meat and broth on the side.
  2. You can also serve it with salsa criolla, a Puerto Rican tomato, onion, peppers, and garlic sauce.
